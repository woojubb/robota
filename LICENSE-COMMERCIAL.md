## When you need a commercial license

The AGPL-3.0 (see [LICENSE](LICENSE)) lets you use Robota freely, including in
production, **provided you comply with its terms** — most notably:

- If you distribute the software, or run a modified version that users interact
  with over a network, you must make the **complete corresponding source code**
  (including your modifications) available to those users under the AGPL-3.0.
- Derivative and combined works must also be licensed under the AGPL-3.0.

A **commercial license** removes the AGPL-3.0 copyleft/source-disclosure
obligations and is intended for organizations that want to:

- Embed Robota in a **proprietary or closed-source** product or service.
- Offer Robota (or a modified version) as a **network/SaaS service** without
  publishing their source code.
- Redistribute Robota inside a closed-source application or SDK.
- Obtain terms (warranty, indemnification, support SLA) not offered by the
  AGPL-3.0.

If none of the above apply (e.g., internal use, or you are happy to comply with
the AGPL-3.0), **you do not need a commercial license** — the AGPL-3.0 already
grants broad rights at no cost.

## What the commercial license grants

A commercial license grants a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to use,
modify, and distribute Robota **without** the AGPL-3.0 source-disclosure and
copyleft obligations, under the negotiated commercial terms. Exact scope,
